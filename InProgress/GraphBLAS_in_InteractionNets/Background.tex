\subsection{Interaction Nets}

Introduce Interaction Nets as a graph-rewriting model. Explain the core concepts: agents (nodes with a principal port and auxiliary ports), active pairs (two agents connected by their principal ports), and interaction rules (rewriting rules that define how active pairs are reduced).

Emphasize the key properties: one-step confluence (the order of reduction doesn't matter) and inherent parallelism (active pairs can be reduced simultaneously).
