Future work includes extending the set of implemented operations and algorithms.
Operations: indexed operations, slices, 
Algorithms: PageRank, minimum spanning forest (MSF), regular path queries, context-free path queries (CFPQ), and maximal independent set (MIS).

A systematic evaluation of other interaction‑net‑based implementations, such as HVM and Vine, is also required.

Alternative sparse matrix representations, such as the coordinate list (COO) format, rely on sorting---an operation that parallelizes well in interaction nets and therefore warrants further investigation.

Kernel fusion presents another direction.
Techniques such natural for functional programming advanced optimization techniques as supercompilation~\cite{!!!} or distillation~\cite{!!!} could be applied to automatically fuse sequences of operations, analogous to manual fusion for masks and other constructs in classic GraphBLAS.
Can they be used for interaction nets?

Finally, hardware acceleration remains an open opportunity.
Designing an FPGA specifically optimized for interaction net reduction could exploit the fine‑grained parallelism inherent to the model and yield substantial performance gains.
